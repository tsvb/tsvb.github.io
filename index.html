Mid Century Modern Architecture and Design

Introduction

Mid Century Modern (MCM) architecture and design emerged in the 1940s and 1950s, representing a design movement that was both innovative and influential. It was a response to the post-war need for affordable, functional housing and commercial spaces that also offered a sense of optimism and style.

Key Elements of Mid Century Modern Design

  • Clean lines and minimal ornamentation
  • Open floor plans
  • Large windows and natural light
  • Indoor-outdoor living
  • Use of new materials, such as plywood, plastic, and molded plexiglass
  • Bold colors and graphic patterns

Influential Architects and Designers

Some of the most influential architects and designers of the Mid Century Modern movement include:

  • Charles and Ray Eames
  • Eero Saarinen
  • George Nelson
  • Harry Bertoia
  • Arthur Elrod
  • Alexander Girard

Preservation and Restoration

Today, Mid Century Modern architecture and design is highly sought after and many buildings and homes have been preserved or restored to their original condition. Organizations such as the Mid Century Modern Society and the Society for Commercial Archaeology work to preserve and promote MCM buildings and design.
